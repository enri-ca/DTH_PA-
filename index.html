About

The "Partizione Antica" of the Supino Art Photo Archival fund - belonging to the Biblioteca delle Arti dell'Alma Mater Studiorum Università di Bologna - is a special subfund that has always been valorized for its stunning photograph images and their documental importance for history of art.

It has been catalogued in the '90s from the Archivio Storico of the University of Bologna, and their data are available in the online public access catalogue Catalogo del Polo Bolognese, and, more recentely, from the Zeri fundation catalogue in the specific scheda F (for photographs) and scheda OA (for works of art) Italian Ministry ICCD rules.

Most of the photographs are handwritten annotated.

»

But, who is that collector?

Despite the Partizione Antica has received a lot of attentions and has been studied, catalogued and digitized, the identity of its creator, remains unknown.

Here a summary of the few known information:

  • Timespan activity: secon hanlf of XIXth century
  • Interested in: Art
  • Language: native English, proficient in French, Italian and German
  • Freelance: Available

So, who am I?

According to the Zeri metadata, the most present work of art type in the picture I collected is sculpture (62%), followed by architecture (26%).

I am really keen on funerary monuments.

The works of art are mainly located in Italy (62%), Greece (12%) and mediterranean area, with an atypical little indian nucleous of 18 photographs.

Most of photographs are from unindentified photograpers (49%), the rest comes from well known photographer's ateliers specialized in history of art such as Alinari (20%), Moscioni, Brogi, Sommer (5% each), Laurent, Incorpora, Giraudon, etc.

For about 100/112 photographers, I have less than 20 photos each.

The anonymous photographs I collected, have been shooted, as the whole collection, in Italy, Grece and mediterranean area.

More than half of the photos (55%) reports my handwritten annotations on the back side.

In 22 of my annotation I reported my thoughts, impressions, experiences: I reported some of my movements (1861 Corneto, 1865 Atene, 1866 Chiusi, 1887 Olympia, 1892 Palermo, 1893 Olympia, 1894 Corfu).
