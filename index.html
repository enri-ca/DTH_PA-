About

The "Partizione Antica" of the Supino Art Photo Archival fund - belonging to the Biblioteca delle Arti dell'Alma Mater Studiorum Universit√† di Bologna - is a special subfund that has always been valorized for its stunning photograph images and their documental importance for history of art.

It has been catalogued in the '90s from the Archivio Storico of the University of Bologna, and their data are available in the online public access catalogue Catalogo del Polo Bolognese, and, more recentely, from the Zeri fundation catalogue in the specific scheda F (for photographs) and scheda OA (for works of art) Italian Ministry ICCD rules.

Most of the photographs are handwritten annotated.

But, who is that collector?

Despite the Partizione Antica has received a lot of attentions and has been studied, catalogued and digitized, the identity of its creator, remains unknown.

The project

The data

The research started from data extraction of the Supino Partizione Antica fund records provided from the Federico Zeri Foundation catalogue: the original data counted 3.260 F records and 2.634 OA records - respectively for the photographs, and for the depicted works of art. The data count on either well structured metadata using controlled vocabularies, as well as unstructured free-text fields.

Usinf both this kind of data, the investigation try to answer some question to better identify our misterious collector:

  • What are the most frequent work of art types? Where the depicted works of art were located?
  • What are the most "used" photographers and where they have their ateliers?
  • Where anonymous photographs have been shot?
  • How many photographs records report transcribed annotations?
  • Which dates entities can be detected in the transcribed annotations?
  • When the annotator have written their notes?
  • Can be detected some "personal experiences" from handwritten annotations? If yes, what?

Data have been enhanced with more informations coming from wikidata sparql queries or geolocation information.
All the process is illustrated in the Jupyter notebook.
The original data have been used for illustrative and didactical purposes only: all the credits and reuse authorizations must be asked to Federico Zeri Foundation.

Who am I?

Works of art

According to the Zeri metadata, the most present work of art typology in the picture I collected is sculpture (62%), followed by architecture (26%).
The works of art are mainly located in Italy (62%), Greece (12%) and mediterranean area, with an atypical little indian nucleous of 18 photographs.

Photographers

Most of my photographs are from unindentified photograpers (49%), the rest comes from well known photographer's ateliers specialized in history of art such as Alinari (20%), Moscioni, Brogi, Sommer (5% each), Laurent, Incorpora, Giraudon, etc. for a total of n. photographers.
Their ateliers are located ....
The anonymous photographs I collected, have been shooted, as the whole collection, in Italy, Grece and mediterranean area but with a higher number of photographs from ...

Notes

More than half of the records (55%) report transcriptions of mine annotation, even if some of them are incomplete (12%).

In x of them I reported, in first person, my thoughts, impressions, experiences: I am quite obsessed by medals; I used to refere to several person... I reported some of my movements (1861 Corneto, 1865 Atene, 1866 Chiusi, 1887 Olympia, 1892 Palermo, 1893 Olympia, 1894 Corfu).
These information togheter with other analysis, confirm that I have been active mainly in the second half of XIXth century.
